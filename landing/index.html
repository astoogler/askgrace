Common Questions

Is Ask Grace really free?

Yes. You can start chatting with Grace right away at no cost. No credit card required. We offer optional premium features for those who want more, but the core experience is free.

Is it safe? Can Grace give bad medical advice?

Grace is specifically designed to never diagnose, prescribe, or give specific medical, legal, or financial advice. She shares general wellness tips and always reminds you to check with your doctor or a qualified professional. Every response is filtered through safety guardrails.

Is my information private?

Your conversations are private. Ask Grace does not sell your personal information. Your data is protected with bank-level security and only you can access your account.

Do I need to download an app?

No. Ask Grace works in your phone or tablet's web browser. You can also add it to your home screen for easy access — it works just like an app without the download.

What if there's an emergency?

Ask Grace has a built-in emergency button that connects you to family or 911 with one touch. Grace also detects urgent language in your messages and guides you to get help immediately.

Can my family see my conversations?

No. Your conversations are private to your account. In the future, we plan to offer optional family sharing features, but only with your explicit permission.

I'm not good with technology. Can I still use it?

Absolutely. Ask Grace was designed specifically for people who find technology challenging. The text is large, the buttons are big, and you can even talk to Grace using your voice instead of typing.
